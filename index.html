About this tool

Overview

This tool calculates the total academic workload for each staff member by combining data from multiple sources. It mirrors the University's Academic Workload Planning (WLP) framework, grouping activities into Teaching, Citizenship, and Research, with a final Combined Totals view that merges everything together.

📚 Teaching

Timetabled teaching load — Parses exported HTML timetables and calculates weekly contact hours per staff member. Hours are calculated from session start/end times. A flat-rate preparation allowance (default 2 hours per timetabled activity) can be added. The Realistic hours option deduplicates overlapping sessions so you are not counted as being in two places at once.

Non-timetabled assessment — Upload the assessment spreadsheet. Total load hours are divided equally among all listed staff, with optional 1.0× or 0.5× extra allowance for staff with unexpired entries.

Research Projects — Upload the project allocation spreadsheet. Calculates supervision (12 hrs/student share), dissertation feedback (3 hrs/student), dissertation marking (2 hrs/assessment), poster feedback (0.5 hr/student), and poster marking (0.333 hr/assessment) for each academic. Co-supervision hours are displayed for information but set to zero.

🏛 Citizenship

Tutorials — Upload the student allocation spreadsheet. Year 1 tutees are allocated 16 hrs each, other-year tutees 8 hrs each. An extra CPD/PDP allowance (default 1 hr per student on selected courses) can be added.

MMIs — Upload the multi-tab MMI spreadsheet. Each tab is one interview session; the tab name encodes the date and time. Staff are listed per session, and total hours are calculated from session duration. Reserve staff are identified and their hours are excluded from workload totals.

School Citizenship — Paste SharePoint tables for Teaching, Research, and School/Citizenship roles. Hours per role are shared equally among named role-holders. &-separated names indicate shared roles.

🔬 Research

Staff research hours — Upload the research hours spreadsheet. Shows FTE, project count, and current/next year hours per staff member. The Combined view uses whichever year you select.

PGR supervision — Upload the PGR supervision spreadsheet. Each PhD/MRes student contributes 100 hours per year, split according to the supervision percentages assigned to each supervisor.

⚡ Combined Totals

The combined view merges names across all sources using fuzzy matching (title-stripped, nickname-aware). Staff appearing in multiple sources are consolidated into a single row. The total for each academic is the sum of:

Teaching Contact hours × (1 + bonus) + prep hours
Non-timetabled assess. Equal share × multiplier
Projects Sum of all project roles × (1 + bonus)
Tutorial Sum of tutee hours per year group
MMI Sum of active session durations
Citizenship Sum of role hours
Research Current/next year hours from spreadsheet
PGR Sum of (100 hrs × supervision share) per student
Academic housekeeping Flat % of FTE target (default 10%)

🎯 FTE & Protected Time

The annual FTE target defaults to 1,600 hours (full-time). Each academic's personal target is 1,600 × effective FTE.

FTE fractions can be set per person via tag rules or a manual override. Common conventions include:

  • 0.75 FTE — Research & Teaching (R&T) staff with 0.25 FTE protected research time. Teaching load target = 1,600 × 0.75 = 1,200 hours.
  • 0.90 FTE — Teaching-only staff with 0.10 FTE scholarship allocation.
  • 1.00 FTE — Full-time Education & Research or Teaching-only staff with no protected time adjustment.

When multiple tag rules with FTE fractions apply to the same person, the fractions multiply (e.g. 0.75 × 0.5 = 0.375 effective FTE). You can also set a manual FTE override per person via the tag popover, which bypasses all tag-based fractions.

The FTE bar in the Combined view compares each academic's total hours against their personal target, colour-coded for under/on/over.

🧹 Academic Housekeeping

A flat-rate overhead (default 10% of the personal FTE target) added to each academic's total. This covers compulsory training, general emails, staff meetings, and other non-specific activities not captured by the main categories. The rate is configurable in the FTE Settings panel (0–50%).

📐 Calculation Notes

  • Tag bonuses are additive multipliers applied on top of global settings. A Teaching Load bonus of +0.5 means 1.5× the contact hours. Prep bonuses add to the global prep rate (e.g. global 2.0 + bonus 0.5 = 2.5 hrs prep per session).
  • Realistic mode — overlapping sessions on the same day are merged so you are not double-counted for simultaneous activities. This applies to timetabled teaching and flows through to the Combined view.
  • Deduplication — identical sessions across different week ranges are counted once per week. Prep hours are calculated per unique session, not per timetable row.
  • Anonymous mode replaces staff names with Flintstones-style pseudonyms for screen sharing or demonstrations.
  • All data stays in your browser. Nothing is uploaded to any server. Settings, tags, and rules are persisted in localStorage and can be exported/imported as a JSON file.
